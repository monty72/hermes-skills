# Creator Case Studies

## Marc Louvion — ShipFast ($95k/mo, $1M+/yr)
**Product:** Next.js boilerplate + dev tools (ShipFast, LogoFast, CodeFast)
**Starting cost:** ~$0 (existing code, 1 week assembly)
**Revenue:** $63k in first 60 days → $95k MRR

**Pattern:** Failed 12 products before hitting ShipFast. Built free micro-tools as marketing funnels (LogoFast: 50k+ creations → $5k indirect revenue). 13/21 Product Hunt launches hit #1. Named Maker of the Year 2023.

**Marketing ($0):** Build-in-public on X (60k followers in ~1yr), Product Hunt launches, engineering-as-marketing ("the product IS the marketing"). No PPC ever.

**Stack:** Next.js, TailwindCSS, Stripe, Vercel

## Andy Cloke — Data Fetcher ($23k/mo, $276k/yr)
**Product:** Airtable extension pulling API data on schedule
**Starting cost:** $100
**Revenue:** $23k MRR, 600 paying users, 75-85% margin

**Pattern:** Built on a fast-growing platform (Airtable). Borrowed UX from Google Sheets connectors. Mapped support tickets to build most-wanted integrations first.

**Marketing ($0):** 70-80% from Airtable marketplace itself. Blog + YouTube tutorials. Never spent on ads.

---

## Gil Hildebrand — Subscribr ($1M ARR in 18 months)
**Product:** AI YouTube scriptwriting tool
**Starting cost:** ~$0
**Revenue:** $20k pre-sales → $10k MRR (100 days) → $83k MRR (18 months)

**Pattern:** Pre-sold $20k in lifetime deals BEFORE writing product code (50 subs from 1,000-email list). Promised delivery in 2 months with refund guarantee. Goldilocks market: YouTube creators — big for $1M, small to avoid VC.

**Marketing:** Build-in-public on X, heavy SEO from day 1, AppSumo campaign

## Pieter Levels — Photo AI / Nomad List / Remote OK ($3M+/yr)
**Products:** Multiple solo-operated products
**Starting cost:** $0
**Revenue:** ~$3M/year total (Photo AI $138k/mo, Remote OK $35k/mo, Interior AI $38k/mo)
**Team size:** 0 — always solo

**Pattern:** "12 startups in 12 months" challenge (2014) — most failed, 3-4 hit. Launch at 70% done, iterate. Anti-perfectionism: "bugs are fine if core works." Vanilla PHP, SQLite, simple VPS.

**Marketing:** Pioneered "build in public" on X. Product Hunt. SEO + organic. Never spent a dollar on ads.

## Tim Bennetto — Pallyy ($85k MRR)
**Product:** Social media management for agencies
**Starting cost:** near $0 (self-taught coder)
**Revenue:** $0 (flop) → $1.3k MRR (stuck 2yr) → $2.5k MRR (pivot) → 10x (SEO) → $85k MRR

**Pattern:** Originally "ShareMyInsights" (Instagram analytics — nobody used it). Pivoted to post scheduling when customers asked — doubled MRR immediately. Niched down to social media agencies.

**Marketing:** First 100 customers from friend redirecting traffic. Instagram DM outreach. SEO + blog = "the turning point."

## Common Patterns (All 6)

| Pattern | Frequency |
|---------|-----------|
| Self-built the product | 6/6 |
| Built in public on X/Twitter | 5/6 |
| Launched on Product Hunt | 5/6 |
| Zero paid ads at start | 6/6 |
| Niched down hard | 6/6 |
| Shipped fast/imperfect MVP | 6/6 |
| Charged real money early | 6/6 |
| Listed to users for direction | 6/6 |

**Actual starting costs:** $0-$100 across all 6. No one spent more than a few dollars on domain/hosting.

**What didn't work:** Paid Instagram/Google ads with tiny budgets, building features nobody asked for, over-broad branding, over-engineering, chasing VC funding.
