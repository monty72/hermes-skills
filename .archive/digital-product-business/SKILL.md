# Digital Product Business

## Overview

Build a digital products business with minimal capital (£0-100 seed) by leveraging AI for creation, deployment, and automation. The core insight: digital products have 70-95% margins, "create once sell forever" scalability, and can be built and shipped by an AI agent while you provide domain expertise and distribution.

## When to Use

- User says "let's make money" or "side hustle"
- User says "digital products marketplace"
- User says "what can I build and sell" with £1k or less
- User wants to monetise domain expertise (cloud architecture, energy, homelab, AI)
- User asks about Gumroad, LemonSqueezy, Etsy, or digital downloads
- Researching what sells online

## The Creator Playbook (From $0-$95k/mo Case Studies)

Every successful solo creator in this space followed the same pattern:

| Pattern | Frequency (6/6 case studies) |
|---|---|
| Self-built the product (no outsourcing) | 6/6 |
| Built in public on X/Twitter | 5/6 |
| Launched on Product Hunt | 5/6 |
| Zero paid ads at start | 6/6 |
| Niched down hard | 6/6 |
| Shipped fast/imperfect MVP | 6/6 |
| Charged real money early | 6/6 |
| Listened to users for direction | 6/6 |

**Actual starting costs:** $0–$100 across all 6 case studies (domain + hosting only).

**What didn't work** (from their own admissions):
- Paid Instagram/Google ads with tiny budgets → waste
- Building features nobody asked for → wasted months
- Over-broad branding → confused customers
- Over-engineering / perfectionism → delayed validation
- Chasing VC funding → "selling your soul"

|---|---|---|---|
| Templates | $5-15 | $19-49 | $49-97 |
| Ebooks/Guides | $4.99-9.99 | $14.99-29.99 | $29.99-49 |
| Code Starters | $19-39 | $39-79 | $79-199 |

| AI Prompt Packs | $9-19 | $19-47 | $47-97 |
| Courses | $19-49 | $49-199 | $199-499 |

**Principle:** Price at the value of the problem solved, not the effort to create. A WAF review toolkit at £29 is a no-brainer for an enterprise architect whose time costs £100+/hr.

## Product Launch Workflow

### Week 1-2: Validate & Build
1. Pick a narrow niche where you have genuine expertise
2. Check Etsy/Gumroad/Reddit for existing products and pain points
3. Build the MVP (I can do this — agent generates files, templates, landing page)
4. Set up payment (LemonSqueezy for UK — handles VAT automatically)

### Week 2-3: Launch
1. Create product page on your site (preview, features, FAQ, screenshots)
2. List on Etsy (for discovery — 95M+ active buyers, ~16% fee)
3. Post on X/Twitter with build-in-public thread
4. Submit to Product Hunt
5. Cross-post to relevant subreddits and LinkedIn

### Week 4+: Iterate
1. Collect feedback from first buyers
2. Update product based on real usage
3. Build second product (4-5 products = avg $82K vs 1 product = $13K)
4. Bundle products at a discount
5. Consider subscription/membership model for recurring revenue

## Pitfalls

1. **Products under £15 get eaten by fees** — On Gumroad, effective rate at £15 product is ~21%. Price £25+ for decent margins.

2. **No built-in audience = use Etsy first** — Without a following on X/LinkedIn, your site gets zero traffic. Etsy's 95M+ buyers provide discovery. Accept the 16% fee as marketing cost.

3. **Single product isn't enough** — Data shows 4-5 products generate $82K avg vs 1 product at $13K. Build a portfolio.

4. **Generic products compete on price** — "Notion templates for project management" is a race to the bottom. "Notion dashboard for Azure WAF reviews" at £29 sells.

5. **VAT/ tax complexity** — LemonSqueezy handles this globally (Merchant of Record). Gumroad does too since Jan 2025. Payhip only handles EU/UK. Don't self-manage tax — use a MoR platform.

6. **Lifetime updates = free forever** — Price accordingly. Bundle "lifetime updates" into the premium tier and offer annual subscriptions for ongoing content.

7. **Fees eat into thin margins** — The 5% LemonSqueezy fee on a £29 product is £1.45. But the Stripe processing on Payhip (2.9% + 30p) on top of their 5% makes it ~£2.64. Compare effective rates, not headline rates.

8. **Notion template delivery** — Don't host files directly. Use the "Duplicate" link pattern: create the template, publish it to Notion's template gallery or share with a public link. The delivery is a URL, not a file.
