# SPV Company Setup for Property Investment

## Step 1: Register the Company

**Companies House** (online, ~20 mins): [companieshouse.gov.uk](https://companieshouse.gov.uk)

| Item | Cost (2026) |
|------|-------------|
| Digital incorporation fee | £100 (was £50 before Feb 2026) |
| Confirmation Statement (annual) | £50 |

**What you need:**
- **Company name** — check availability on Companies House + IPO trademark register. Pattern: `{YourName} Properties Ltd` or `{YourName} Holdings Ltd`
- **SIC code** — **68209** (Other letting and operating of own or leased real estate). This is the code lenders expect for a BTL SPV
- **Registered office** — can be your home or an accountant's address (pay service for privacy)
- **Director** — you (or joint with partner)
- **Shareholder** — you. Consider alphabet shares later for tax-efficient dividend distribution to family

**For flipping** (buy-refurb-sell): use SIC code **68100** (Buying and selling of own real estate). Keep flip and BTL in separate companies — lenders prefer clean SPVs.

## Step 4: Get an SPV Mortgage

**SPV BTL vs Personal BTL comparison:**

| | Personal | SPV |
|---|---|---|
| Typical rate (Jun 2026) | 4.5-5.5% | 5.0-6.5% |
| LTV available | Up to 75% | Up to 75% |
| Rate premium | — | +0.5-1.0% |

**Lenders that do SPV BTL:** Together, Fleet, Aldermore, Foundation. Use a specialist broker.

**Bridging loan** (for initial purchase + refurb phase, 6-12 month term):
- Rate: ~0.5-0.8% per month (interest typically rolled up, not paid monthly)
- LTV: Up to 75% of purchase price
- After refurb, refinance onto a BTL mortgage

**Recommended broker:** Fox Davidson (foxdavidson.co.uk) — specialist in SPV BTL mortgages.

## Key Questions to Ask Before Buying Your First SPV Property

1. **Agent:** Lease years remaining? Ground rent? Subletting restrictions?
2. **Mortgage broker:** Pre-approval for SPV bridging + BTL — know your rate before you offer
3. **Accountant:** SPV already registered, registered address confirmed, year-end date set
4. **Solicitor:** Experience with SPV purchases — they need to know the company buys, not you
