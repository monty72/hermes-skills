## SPV vs Personal — Tax Comparison for Higher-Rate Taxpayers

### Personal Name (40% taxpayer)
- Rental profit taxed at 40% income tax
- Mortgage interest relief RESTRICTED to 20% basic-rate tax credit (Section 24)
- net: you pay tax on gross rent minus expenses, even if mortgage payments exceed profit
- CGT on sale: 24% (higher rate)

### Limited Company (SPV)
- Rental profit taxed at 19% corporation tax (up to £50k profit)
- Full mortgage interest deduction as business expense
- Can retain profits in company tax-efficiently
- Dividend tax on extraction: 10.75% (basic) / 35.75% (higher) — increased 2% from Apr 2026
- CGT replaced by corporation tax on gain (19-25%)
- Extra admin: ~£500-1k/yr accountant, confirmation statement, annual accounts

### When personal wins
- Basic-rate taxpayer only
- 1-2 properties only, need all cashflow extracted
- Plan to sell within 5 years (CGT lower than CT + dividend extraction)

### When SPV wins
- Higher-rate (40%) or additional-rate (45%) taxpayer
- Building a portfolio (3+ properties)
- Can leave profits in company to reinvest
- Plans to hold long-term and pass via shares (IHT planning)

## The Golden Rule
**Buy into the company from day one.** Transferring personally-owned property into a company later triggers SDLT at market value (3% surcharge) + CGT at 24% on any gain + legal fees. The middle path (buy now, transfer later) is the most expensive option.
